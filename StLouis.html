Jefferson City

Missouri's largest city, St. Louis, was built near the ancient city of the Cahokian Mound Builders. St. Louis was founded to help the fur trade down the Mississippi. Purchased as part of the Louisiana Purchase in 1803, St. Louis became the unofficial capital of America's new lands. In 1877, the city was formally separated from the county to save money, a decision that has never been reversed.

St. Louis

Also known by its city code, StL, and nickname, The Mound City.


Fun Facts:

  • It was known as the gateway to the west because it was the last major city on the Oregon Trail.
  • Founded at the convergence of two of America's greatest rivers, the Missouri and the Mississippi.
  • Founded under the French, ceded to the Spanish, given back to the French, and sold to the Americans.
  • Has the biggest free zoo in America.
  • It was the fourth-largest city in the US between 1870 and 1920.
  • Held the 1904 Summer Olympics.
